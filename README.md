I used three parallel reviews over the backend, case/grouping flow, and mobile/dashboard UX. The short version is: your repo has good foundations, but it is still built as an `AI-assisted reporting system with manual police review`, not the `AI-primary verification + automatic incident consolidation platform` described in your proposal.

**Main Gaps**

- The biggest architectural gap is that automatic same-incident grouping and automatic case creation do not exist. Cases are still created manually through [cases.py](/d:/final year project/TrustbondMobileApp/backend/app/api/v1/cases.py) and the dashboard UI in [CaseManagement.jsx](/d:/final year project/TrustbondMobileApp/frontend/src/components/screens/CaseManagement.jsx). After AI verification, reports only trigger hotspot recomputation in [reports.py](/d:/final year project/TrustbondMobileApp/backend/app/api/v1/reports.py); they do not flow into `IncidentGroup -> Case`.
- `IncidentGroup` is present in name but not operationally integrated. The model in [incident_group.py](/d:/final year project/TrustbondMobileApp/backend/app/models/incident_group.py) is not linked back to reports or cases, and the API in [incident_groups.py](/d:/final year project/TrustbondMobileApp/backend/app/api/v1/incident_groups.py) is just a read-only listing.
- Your proposal says the authenticity model is Random Forest, but the implemented report model is XGBoost in [train_report_credibility_model.py](/d:/final year project/TrustbondMobileApp/backend/musanze/train_report_credibility_model.py) and runtime scoring in [credibility_model.py](/d:/final year project/TrustbondMobileApp/backend/app/core/credibility_model.py). The only Random Forest found is for hotspot cluster classification, not report authenticity.
- The backend is not yet designed for “AI is fully in charge of verification.” It can auto-verify some reports, but many paths still intentionally leave reports in `under_review` or require manual review in [reports.py](/d:/final year project/TrustbondMobileApp/backend/app/api/v1/reports.py) and [report_priority.py](/d:/final year project/TrustbondMobileApp/backend/app/core/report_priority.py).
- The hotspot pipeline is not a true trust-weighted DBSCAN. In [hotspot_auto.py](/d:/final year project/TrustbondMobileApp/backend/app/core/hotspot_auto.py), trust is mostly used as filtering and post-cluster scoring, not as a real weight inside DBSCAN density formation.

**Incomplete Or Only Partially Implemented**

- Much of the AI-assisted evidence verification exists as fields, not working execution. [evidence_file.py](/d:/final year project/TrustbondMobileApp/backend/app/models/evidence_file.py) has `blur_score`, `tamper_score`, `quality_label`, and `ai_checked_at`, but the upload flow in [reports.py](/d:/final year project/TrustbondMobileApp/backend/app/api/v1/reports.py) does not compute them. It also stores an exact SHA-256 hash, not real perceptual hashing.
- The proposed Celery/Redis background architecture is not implemented. [requirements.txt](/d:/final year project/TrustbondMobileApp/backend/requirements.txt) has neither, and the app uses inline scoring plus FastAPI `BackgroundTasks`. There is also a concrete bug in [hotspots.py](/d:/final year project/TrustbondMobileApp/backend/app/api/v1/hotspots.py): `recompute_hotspots` calls `background_tasks.add_task(...)` without receiving `background_tasks`.
- Privacy is weaker than the proposal suggests. The mobile app derives `device_hash` from stable device identifiers in [device_service.dart](/d:/final year project/TrustbondMobileApp/TrustBond/lib/services/device_service.dart), stores it persistently, and the backend logs `device_hash`, IP, and user agent through [reports.py](/d:/final year project/TrustbondMobileApp/backend/app/api/v1/reports.py) and [audit_log.py](/d:/final year project/TrustbondMobileApp/backend/app/models/audit_log.py). That is pseudonymous, but not strong unlinkable anonymity.
- The mobile offline system is split between two queue designs. Submission now goes through the newer SQLite-backed integration in [offline_integration_guide.dart](/d:/final year project/TrustbondMobileApp/TrustBond/lib/services/offline_integration_guide.dart), but other screens still rely on the older queue in [offline_report_queue.dart](/d:/final year project/TrustbondMobileApp/TrustBond/lib/services/offline_report_queue.dart). That means offline status can drift from reality.
- The mobile reporting UI does not fully match the proposal’s evidence story. [report_step3_screen.dart](/d:/final year project/TrustbondMobileApp/TrustBond/lib/screens/report_step3_screen.dart) supports camera photo, camera video, and gallery image, but not audio capture, even though the backend accepts audio-like media handling.
- Several privacy/settings features are placeholder UX. In [settings_screen.dart](/d:/final year project/TrustbondMobileApp/TrustBond/lib/screens/settings_screen.dart), actions like “Clear All Data” and “Export My Data” are not fully implemented.

**Not Designed The Way You Proposed**

- The police dashboard is still centered on officer-by-officer review. [ReportDetail.jsx](/d:/final year project/TrustbondMobileApp/frontend/src/components/screens/ReportDetail.jsx) prominently exposes manual `Verify report` and `Flag report`, and [Reports.jsx](/d:/final year project/TrustbondMobileApp/frontend/src/components/screens/Reports.jsx) still treats pending review as a normal workflow.
- Unified case management is manual, not automatic. [NewCaseModal.jsx](/d:/final year project/TrustbondMobileApp/frontend/src/components/Modals/NewCaseModal.jsx) and [LinkCaseModal.jsx](/d:/final year project/TrustbondMobileApp/frontend/src/components/Modals/LinkCaseModal.jsx) ask humans to choose reports and form cases themselves.
- The current “related reports” feature in [reports.py](/d:/final year project/TrustbondMobileApp/backend/app/api/v1/reports.py) is only a loose browse helper. It uses same incident type, same village, and a wide time window; it is not a strict same-place/same-time/different-device consolidation algorithm.
- Product copy is ahead of implementation in several places, especially around privacy guarantees, AI completeness, and offline reliability in [home_screen.dart](/d:/final year project/TrustbondMobileApp/TrustBond/lib/screens/home_screen.dart), [privacy_security_screen.dart](/d:/final year project/TrustbondMobileApp/TrustBond/lib/screens/privacy_security_screen.dart), and [SafetyMap.jsx](/d:/final year project/TrustbondMobileApp/frontend/src/components/screens/SafetyMap.jsx).

Bottom line: your system is already a solid prototype for anonymous reporting, ML-assisted trust scoring, hotspots, and manual case handling. But the proposal you wrote describes a more advanced architecture than the repo currently implements. The most important missing chain is:

`verified report -> incident grouping across devices/time/location -> automatic case creation/update -> case-first police workflow`
